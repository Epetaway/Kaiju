Earl the Kaiju

I am someone who feels a deep connection to the concept of kaiju. For those who may not know, a kaiju is a giant monster that often appears in Japanese science fiction films. But beyond the surface level imagery, I relate to the idea of a kaiju as a powerful force that can cause destruction, but also has the potential for great good. My journey to Jiu-jitsu began in 2015 when I felt lost and uncertain about my place in the world. As an only child raised by independent women, I struggled with confidence and self-esteem issues. Growing up, I loved martial arts and found Karate to be the only sport I was allowed to do, which I loved. However, I longed for the connection and community that martial arts provided, but it seemed like it wasn't in the cards for me. Jiu-jitsu changed all of that. Through this art, I was able to channel my inner kaiju and embrace my strength and power, while also learning control and discipline. Jiu-jitsu has given me the tools to be the father and the man I never had in my life, and I am forever grateful for this art and the community that comes with it.
